Cha[n]

Negative declarative form of copula

Subject is (emphatic) personal pronoun

  • Cha mhise Iain. ‘I am not John.’
  • Chan ise Peigi. ‘She is not Peggy.’
  • Cha sinne na tidsearan. ‘We are not the teachers.’
  • Chan esan mo bhràthair. ‘He is not my brother.’

Subject is definite noun phrase

  • Chan i/e Anna an tidsear. ‘Ann is not the teacher.’
  • Chan e mo athair am ministear. ‘My father is not the minister.’
  • Chan e an sagart do bhràthair. ‘The priest is not your brother.’
  • Chan e seo an tidsear. ‘This is not the teacher.’
  • Chan i/e siud Màiri. ‘That is not Mary.’

cf.

  • Chan e mise Iain. ‘I am not John.’
  • Chan e ise Peigi. ‘She is not Peggy.’
  • Chan e sinne na tidsearan. ‘We are not the teachers.’
  • Chan e esan mo bhràthair. ‘He is not my brother.’

Takes dependent form of verb

  • Chan eil mi sgìth. ‘I am not tired.’
  • Chan eil cat aig Màiri. ‘Mary does not have a cat.’
